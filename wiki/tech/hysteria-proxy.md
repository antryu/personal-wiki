# Hysteria Proxy

> Hysteria is a powerful, lightning fast, and censorship-resistant proxy built to work reliably over unstable networks. It leverages a customized QUIC protocol and supports multiple modes like SOCKS5, HTTP Proxy, TUN, and more. It is notable for its high performance, strong privacy features, and ability to bypass network restrictions.
> Last updated: 2026-05-11

## Overview
Hysteria is a next-generation proxy developed by AperNet that is designed to overcome limitations of traditional protocols. It uses a custom version of QUIC to achieve high performance on unreliable and lossy networks, making it ideal for environments with network restrictions or instability.

The project supports a wide range of protocols and modes, including SOCKS5, HTTP Proxy, TCP/UDP forwarding, Linux TProxy, TUN, and more. Its ability to masquerade as HTTP/3 traffic makes it difficult for censorship systems to detect and block its use. This, combined with its cross-platform support and ease of integration, makes Hysteria a versatile and robust solution for users and developers alike.

Hysteria is actively maintained and has a strong community, with detailed documentation and third-party applications to extend its functionality.

## Key Points
- **Censorship-resistant**: Uses HTTP/3-like traffic patterns to evade detection.
- **Lightning fast performance**: Built using QUIC for speed and reliability.
- **Cross-platform support**: Works on all major operating systems and architectures.
- **Easy to integrate**: Offers customizable authentication and access control.
- **Developer-friendly**: Open source (MIT license) with good documentation for contributions.
