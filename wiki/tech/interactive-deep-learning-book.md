# Interactive Deep Learning Book

> An open-source, interactive deep learning book with multi-framework code, math, and discussions. Adopted by 500 universities across 70 countries, including Stanford, MIT, and Cambridge, it emphasizes hands-on learning through Jupyter notebooks.  
> Last updated: 2026-04-19

## Overview  
The **D2L.ai** repository hosts an interactive deep learning textbook designed to make the field accessible through practical, runnable code and theoretical explanations. The book is written entirely in Jupyter notebooks, integrating figures, mathematical derivations, and interactive examples. It supports multiple frameworks, including PyTorch, TensorFlow, JAX, and MXNet, enabling readers to experiment with real-world implementations.  

Key features include:  
- **Educational adoption**: Used in courses at 500+ universities, such as UC Berkeley’s STAT 157 and courses at Stanford, MIT, and Harvard.  
- **Community-driven updates**: Over 5,000 forks and 201 open issues highlight active contributions and iterative improvements.  
- **Endorsements**: Praised by leaders like NVIDIA’s Jensen Huang and academic experts for balancing theory and practice.  
- **Topics covered**: Computer vision, NLP, reinforcement learning, Gaussian processes, and hyperparameter optimization.  

The repository includes a forum for discussions, a BibTeX citation for academic use, and guidelines for contributing. It was last updated on April 19, 2026, and maintains a "Other" license.  

## Key Points  
- Combines Jupyter notebooks with self-contained code, math, and visualizations for interactive learning.  
- Supports multiple deep learning frameworks (PyTorch, TensorFlow, JAX, MXNet).  
- Adopted by 500 universities in 70 countries, reflecting its educational impact.  
- Open-source with active community contributions and a focus on rapid updates.
