# Nomad Workload Orchestrator

> Nomad is an easy-to-use, flexible, and performant workload orchestrator that can deploy a mix of microservice, batch, containerized, and non-containerized applications. It is notable for its simplicity, resilience, and native integration with HashiCorp tools like Consul and Vault.
> Last updated: 2026-05-13

## Overview
Nomad is a workload orchestrator developed by HashiCorp that enables the deployment and management of a wide variety of workloads, including containers (Docker, Podman), non-containerized applications (executable, Java), and virtual machines (QEMU). It is designed to run on Linux, Windows, and macOS, and supports both on-premises and cloud environments. Nomad is known for its ease of use, resilience, and ability to scale globally with federation support.

Nomad simplifies the orchestration of containers, legacy applications, and batch jobs by combining resource management and scheduling in a single system. It does not require external services for storage or coordination and is designed to handle failures automatically. In addition to container and batch support, it provides GPU and device plugin support for machine learning and AI workloads.

A commercial version, **Nomad Enterprise**, is available for more advanced use cases. Nomad integrates seamlessly with other HashiCorp products such as Terraform, Consul, and Vault for provisioning, service discovery, and secrets management.

## Key Points
- Nomad is a workload orchestrator that supports containers, non-containerized applications, and virtual machines.
- It is designed to be simple and reliable, with no external dependencies.
- Nomad supports GPU and device plugins, making it ideal for machine learning and AI.
- It provides out-of-the-box federation capabilities for multi-region and multi-cloud deployments.
- Nomad is part of the HashiCorp ecosystem and integrates with Consul, Vault, and Terraform.
