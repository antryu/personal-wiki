# Muesli: Local Speech-to-Text Tool for macOS

> Muesli is a lightweight, native macOS app that enables local-first dictation and meeting transcription. It offers an alternative to tools like Granola and WisprFlow, with on-device speech-to-text, zero cloud costs, and strong privacy features.  
> Last updated: 2026-05-13

## Overview

Muesli is a **local-first dictation and meeting transcription tool** developed for macOS. It provides high-quality transcription without sending any data to the cloud, ensuring privacy and reducing costs. Built with Swift and leveraging Apple Silicon and CoreML for performance, it supports a variety of speech recognition models and advanced features like speaker diarization and meeting detection.

Key highlights include **zero-cloud transcription**, **hands-free dictation**, **meeting recording with speaker separation**, and **integration with Google Calendar** for automatic meeting recognition and notifications.

Muesli is open-source and released under the MIT License. It is actively maintained, with the latest update on 2026-05-13. The app supports macOS 14.2+ and is optimized for Apple Silicon.

## Key Points

- **Privacy by design**: All transcription runs locally on the device, and no audio leaves unless explicitly configured.
- **High-performance models**: Supports Parakeet TDT, WhisperKit, Cohere Transcribe, and Qwen3 ASR.
- **Meeting transcription**: Captures system and mic audio with speaker diarization and real-time transcription.
