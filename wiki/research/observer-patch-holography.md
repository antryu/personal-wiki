# Observer Patch Holography

> Observer Patch Holography (OPH) is an active research program that aims to construct a fundamental theory of physics based on the principle of observer consistency. It explores how physics can be reconstructed from the idea that each observer only accesses a local patch of reality, and these patches must agree on overlaps. OPH is notable for its attempt to unify general relativity, quantum mechanics, and the Standard Model within a finite holographic framework.
> Last updated: 2026-04-10

## Overview
Observer Patch Holography (OPH) is a reconstruction program for fundamental physics grounded in the idea that no observer can access the whole world at once. Each observer is limited to a local "patch," and neighboring patches must agree on their overlaps. OPH derives spacetime, gauge structure, particles, records, and observer synchronization from this principle, using a finite holographic screen and an explicit set of axioms and branch premises.

The framework delivers a finite-resolution theorem package for observer patches, collars, and overlap repair, and explores conditional routes to Lorentzian geometry, modular time, and Einstein dynamics. It also provides a path to the Standard Model via compact gauge structures and a particle program with exact structural massless carriers. OPH includes a concrete microphysics architecture that integrates measurement, records, and observers into the theory itself.

A local unification surface is constructed around the calibrated UV input, unifying electroweak and gravitational scales. The full OPH derivation stack, from axioms to relativity and particle physics, is visualized and documented in the repository.

## Key Points
- OPH reconstructs physics from the principle of observer patch consistency and finite holography.
- It offers conditional routes to Lorentzian geometry, modular time, and Einstein dynamics.
- It provides a compact gauge route to the Standard Model, including exact hypercharge and particle mass predictions.
- OPH features a particle program with exact massless carriers and a quantitative Higgs/top stage.
- The framework unifies electroweak and gravitational scales via a local unification surface.
