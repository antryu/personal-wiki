# Cost-Inclusive Loss

> Cost-Inclusive Loss is a financial metric that accounts for all direct and indirect costs associated with an investment or business activity when calculating the total loss incurred. It ensures a comprehensive evaluation by including expenses such as transaction fees, taxes, operational costs, and other overheads. Last updated: 2026-04-15.

## Overview
Cost-Inclusive Loss provides a more accurate representation of financial performance by factoring in all costs linked to an activity. Unlike basic loss calculations, which may omit operational or transactional expenses, this approach integrates these elements to reflect the true economic impact. It is particularly relevant in scenarios like asset sales, project closures, or business restructuring, where hidden costs can significantly affect profitability. By incorporating these costs, stakeholders can make informed decisions about risk management, resource allocation, and strategic planning.

## Key Points
- Accounts for all costs (e.g., transaction fees, taxes, operational expenses) in loss calculations.  
- Used to assess the real financial impact of investments or business activities.
