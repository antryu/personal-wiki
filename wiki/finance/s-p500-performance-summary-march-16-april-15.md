# S&P500 Performance Summary: March 16 - April 15

> The S&P 500 recorded a modest gain of 2.3% during the period from March 16 to April 15, driven by resilience in the technology and consumer discretionary sectors amid fluctuating macroeconomic data. Volatility remained elevated due to uncertainty around interest rate decisions and geopolitical tensions.  
> Last updated: 2026-04-15

## Overview
The S&P 500 experienced a 2.3% total return over the 31-day period, with notable intraday swings reflecting mixed market sentiment. Technology stocks, particularly AI-related equities, outperformed, while utilities and real estate lagged. Key economic reports, including mixed inflation data and employment figures, influenced investor behavior. The index closed above 5,200 for the first time since early 2024, supported by strong earnings from major market-cap leaders.

## Key Points
- Technology sector (+4.1%) led gains, fueled by AI infrastructure demand and strong Q1 earnings.
- Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and China-Taiwan Strait contributed to periodic selloffs in energy and defense stocks.
