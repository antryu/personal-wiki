# Big Cycle — 6 Stages (75-100 Year Framework)

> Source: Ray Dalio, *Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order* (2021)
> This document encodes Dalio's framework for analytical use. Not investment advice.

---

## Overview

Dalio observes that empires and reserve-currency nations move through a recurring arc of roughly 75-100 years. The cycle is driven by the interplay of wealth creation, debt accumulation, internal social stress, and external geopolitical rivalry. The same pattern appears across the Dutch, British, and American empires.

---

## Stage 1 — New Order

### Defining Characteristics
- Emerges after a major war, revolution, or systemic crisis that discredits the old regime
- New rules of the game are established (political, monetary, financial)
- Old debts are restructured or wiped; balance sheet is clean
- Power is concentrated in a single dominant actor (nation, coalition, or class)
- Social cohesion is high because sacrifice was recent and the enemy is shared
- Institutions are rebuilt with legitimacy

### Key Indicators (Quantifiable)
| Indicator | Typical Range at Stage Entry |
|---|---|
| Government debt / GDP | Low (often post-restructuring, < 50% for victor) |
| Inflation | Stabilizing after war/crisis spike |
| Currency regime | New anchor established (gold, dollar, etc.) |
| Inequality (Gini) | Compressed (war destroyed wealth at top) |
| Internal conflict index | Declining from peak |
| Productivity growth | Accelerating |

**Data sources**: IMF Historical Public Debt Database; Maddison Project (GDP); World Inequality Database (WID); Uppsala Conflict Data Program

### Asset Class Performance
| Asset Class | Performance | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Equities | Strong (recovering) | Valuation floor reset; growth expectations rising |
| Bonds | Strong | Rates falling from crisis highs; new monetary order restores confidence |
| Gold/Precious Metals | Weak/Neutral | Crisis premium fades as new order stabilizes |
| Commodities | Mixed | Rebuilding demand; but supply also recovering |
| Real Estate | Recovering | Destroyed or undervalued during crisis; mean-reverts |
| Crypto (modern analog) | N/A historically | Speculative; likely treated as "new order" speculative asset in current cycle |

### Historical Examples
- **Dutch** (1600s): After Spanish Armada collapse and formation of VOC — new financial order centered on Amsterdam
- **British** (1815-1820): Post-Napoleonic settlement; Pax Britannica begins; Bank of England entrenched
- **US** (1945-1950): Bretton Woods; USD = world reserve; Marshall Plan; NATO
- **China** (1978-): Deng Xiaoping reforms; new internal order after Cultural Revolution destruction

### Andrew Portfolio Implication
- **Benefits**: PLTU (defense/intelligence demand in reconstruction), NVDX (infrastructure tech), KR derivative ETFs (if Korean positioning post-regional crisis)
- **Suffers**: AGQ, UGL (precious metals fade as crisis premium dissipates), BITX (crypto speculative premium compresses)
- **Action framing**: In Stage 1, rotate from crisis hedges (gold, silver, crypto) into productivity plays (tech, infrastructure, equities). *Framework signal, not advice.*

## Stage 2 — Peace & Prosperity

### Defining Characteristics
- Dominant power sets global rules; rivals accept the order
- Debt levels low relative to income; credit expands productively
- Technological innovation accelerates (industrial revolution, computing, AI)
- Middle class expands; inequality moderate
- Trade globalization deepens under the hegemon's protection
- Financial system is relatively simple and sound

### Key Indicators (Quantifiable)
| Indicator | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Real GDP growth | 3-5%+ for leading nation |
| Debt / GDP | Rising but from low base (50-80% range) |
| Innovation output (patents) | Rising sharply |
| Trade as % of GDP | Expanding |
| Income inequality | Moderate (Gini 0.30-0.38) |
| Military conflict index | Low (hegemon maintains peace) |

### Asset Class Performance
| Asset Class | Performance | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Equities | Very Strong | Earnings growth + low rates + innovation premium |
| Bonds | Moderate (stable) | Low inflation; rates stable |
| Gold/Precious Metals | Weak | No inflation fear; opportunity cost high |
| Commodities | Moderate | Demand growing but supply keeps pace |
| Real Estate | Strong | Wealth effect; urbanization |
| Crypto | N/A / Weak | Low systemic fear; crypto thrives on distrust |

### Historical Examples
- **British** (1820-1870): Railway era; industrialization; pound sterling dominance
- **US** (1950-1965): Post-war boom; suburban expansion; S&P tripled
- **US tech** (1982-2000): Disinflation + computing revolution → secular bull market

### Andrew Portfolio Implication
- **Benefits**: NVDX (AI/compute), PLTU (defense innovation), IONQ/RGTI (quantum, early positioning), COIN (crypto infrastructure as tech)
- **Suffers**: AGQ/UGL/SIL (gold/silver underperform in benign environment), OIH/XLE (energy less critical when growth is innovation-driven)
- **Action framing**: Stage 2 is core tech accumulation phase. Innovation leaders compound. *Framework signal.*

## Stage 3 — Excess

### Defining Characteristics
- Success breeds complacency; debt levels rise significantly
- Asset bubbles form (equities, real estate, financial engineering)
- "This time is different" narratives emerge
- Inequality widens sharply (capital > labor returns)
- Political populism rises on both left and right
- Hegemon begins over-extending militarily and financially
- Credit expansion shifts from productive to speculative uses

### Key Indicators (Quantifiable)
| Indicator | Typical Signal |
|---|---|
| Debt / GDP | 100%+ and accelerating |
| Cyclically-adjusted P/E (CAPE) | > 25-30 |
| Corporate debt / GDP | Historically high |
| Gini coefficient | Rising (0.40+) |
| Current account deficit (hegemon) | Widening |
| Financial sector % of GDP | Elevated |
| Asset price / income ratios | Multi-decade highs |

### Asset Class Performance
| Asset Class | Performance | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Equities | Strong (late cycle) | Momentum + leverage; but fragile |
| Bonds | Weak/Negative | Inflation beginning; rates rising |
| Gold/Precious Metals | Beginning to rise | Smart money hedge building |
| Commodities | Strong (energy, oil) | Demand overheating |
| Real Estate | Very Strong | Bubble territory |
| Crypto | Strong | Speculative excess + inflation hedge narrative |

### Historical Examples
- **US 1920s**: Stock market mania; debt-financed consumption; inequality at Gilded Age highs
- **British 1870-1900**: Industrial overextension; financial crises; Suez Canal crisis
- **US 2000s**: Housing bubble; financial engineering; NINJA loans

**Dalio's estimated current position**: The US is arguably in Stage 3→4 transition as of 2024-2026. Federal debt > 120% GDP; CAPE ~35; wealth inequality at 1929 analog levels. *This is Dalio's framework interpretation, not a prediction.*

### Andrew Portfolio Implication
- **Benefits**: OIH/XLE (energy demand peaks in excess), NVDX (momentum), BITX/ETHU (crypto mania), Real estate (if held)
- **Suffers**: Long bonds (inflation rising), cash (purchasing power declining)
- **Action framing**: Late Stage 3 = trim equity beta, begin building inflation hedges. *Framework signal.*

## Stage 4 — Top

### Defining Characteristics
- Peak of financial and geopolitical excess
- First major cracks appear (debt service problems, social unrest)
- Rival powers challenge the hegemon's position
- Central bank and government policy becomes reactive, not proactive
- Monetary stimulus deployed to postpone reckoning
- Public trust in institutions erodes
- Volatility spikes; "normal" no longer functions

### Key Indicators (Quantifiable)
| Indicator | Typical Signal at Top |
|---|---|
| Central bank balance sheet | Expanding rapidly (QE deployed) |
| Yield curve | Inverted or flat |
| Credit spreads | Widening |
| USD (for US hegemon) | Weakening trend begins |
| Social conflict index | Elevated |
| Geopolitical rival GDP / hegemon GDP | Approaching parity |
| Money printing rate | Accelerating |

### Historical Examples
- **British** (~1910-1914): Imperial overstretch; Entente Cordiale as defensive alliance; gold standard strained
- **US** (~1971): Nixon closes gold window (dollar devaluation); Vietnam overstretch
- **US analog today**: Dalio cites ~2020-2025 as potential US "top" — debt monetization, China near-parity, internal political fracture

### Andrew Portfolio Implication
- **Benefits**: AGQ/UGL/SIL (hard money), BITX/ETHU (crypto as distrust asset), NTR (food security), OIH/XLE (commodities before the break)
- **Suffers**: NVDX (growth stocks vulnerable to rate/confidence shock), long bonds, financial sector
- **Action framing**: Stage 4 = shift portfolio toward hard assets and crisis hedges. *Framework signal.*

## Stage 5 — Decline

### Defining Characteristics
- Debt restructuring inevitable; the question is how (inflation, default, or austerity)
- Currency debasement accelerates
- Social conflict deepens (class, ethnic, political)
- Geopolitical rivalry becomes active conflict (proxy wars → direct)
- The hegemon loses reserve currency premium gradually
- Capital flees to next order assets
- Institutional credibility collapses (courts, press, central bank independence)

### Key Indicators (Quantifiable)
| Indicator | Typical Signal |
|---|---|
| CPI/Inflation | Elevated (4-10%+) or volatile |
| Real interest rates | Negative (financial repression) |
| Currency (real effective exchange rate) | Declining multi-year trend |
| Gold price | Rising (secular bull) |
| Political polarization index | Near historic highs |
| Geopolitical conflict index | Rising |
| Brain drain / capital outflow | Measurable |

### Asset Class Performance
| Asset Class | Performance | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Equities | Volatile / Negative real returns | Inflation erodes; uncertainty premium rises |
| Bonds | Very Poor | Financial repression; inflation destroys real value |
| Gold/Precious Metals | **Very Strong** | Classic decline stage beneficiary |
| Silver | Strong | Industrial + monetary demand |
| Commodities | Strong | Inflation + supply disruption |
| Real Estate (hard assets) | Mixed | Inflation hedge but illiquid; depends on location |
| Crypto | Strong-Volatile | Distrust of fiat; but also risk-off selling episodes |
| Cash | Worst | Real purchasing power destruction |

### Historical Examples
- **British Decline** (1914-1945): Two world wars; sterling devaluation; gold standard abandoned 1931
- **US 1970s**: Nixon shock → stagflation → dollar decline until Volcker shock 1981

### Andrew Portfolio Implication
- **Core beneficiaries**: UGL (2x gold), AGQ (2x silver), SIL (silver miners), NTR (food security), BITX (bitcoin as digital gold), OIH/XLE (commodity inflation)
- **Risk assets**: NVDX, IONQ, RGTI — high beta; consider sizing reduction
- **Action framing**: This is the stage Andrew's portfolio is most explicitly positioned for. Framework validates current overweight in precious metals + crypto. *Framework signal.*

## Stage 6 — Bottom / Restructuring

### Defining Characteristics
- Old debts are wiped (deflation/default/war destruction/hyperinflation)
- New social and political order emerges from chaos
- Winner of the geopolitical contest begins Stage 1 of next cycle
- Extreme asset price depression before recovery
- Generational wealth transfer (from old holders to new)

### Key Indicators
| Indicator | Signal at Bottom |
|---|---|
| Debt/GDP | Collapsed (via default, inflation, or jubilee) |
| Asset valuations | Generationally cheap (CAPE < 10) |
| Geopolitical settlement | Treaty / new international order |
| New currency regime | Established |
| Social cohesion | Recovering after crisis unity |

### Historical Examples
- **Post-WWI** (1918-1924): Weimar hyperinflation (Germany), restructuring; new international order attempt (League of Nations)
- **Post-WWII** (1945): Bretton Woods; dollar standard; IMF/World Bank
- **Future analog**: Dalio does not predict timing; estimates ~2030-2040 as potential US restructuring window

### Andrew Portfolio Implication
- **Survive to benefit**: Maintaining liquidity and hard assets through Stage 5-6 transition positions the holder to buy distressed productive assets at generational lows
- **Beneficiaries at bottom**: Equities of future dominant power, new-order tech, commodities of reconstruction
- **Action framing**: Stage 6 is the "buy everything" moment — but only if you survived Stage 5. *Framework signal.*
