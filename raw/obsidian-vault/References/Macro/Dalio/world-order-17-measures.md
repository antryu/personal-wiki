# World Order — 17 Measures of Empire Strength

> Source: Ray Dalio, *Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order* (2021)
> Scores and trends below are rough estimates from open public data (2024-2026 window).
> All scores are relative/qualitative. Dalio's own composite indices are proprietary.
> This document is an analytical framework tool, not investment advice.

---

## Scoring Convention

Each measure scored **1-10** (10 = dominant global position, 1 = collapsed/negligible).
Trend: **↑ Rising / → Stable / ↓ Declining**

---

## Measure 1 — Education

### Definition
Breadth and quality of the population's education, particularly in science, engineering, and critical thinking. Dalio emphasizes this as the foundation of long-run competitive advantage.

### How to Quantify
- Primary/secondary enrollment rates (UNESCO)
- PISA scores (OECD) — reading, math, science
- Higher education completion rate
- Top university density (QS/THE rankings)
- Public education spending % GDP

### Scores (2024-2026 estimate)

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 6/10 | ↓ | Strong elite universities; K-12 PISA scores mediocre (rank ~25); funding inequality |
| China | 7/10 | ↑ | PISA top performer; STEM focus; rural gap narrows; access expanding |
| Korea | 8/10 | → | Among highest PISA globally; over-credentialization risk; demographic pressure |

---

## Measure 2 — Inventiveness / Technology

### Definition
Capacity to generate new ideas, technologies, and productivity-enhancing innovations. Correlates with patent output, R&D investment, and breakthrough firm creation.

### How to Quantify
- R&D spending % GDP (OECD/World Bank)
- Patent applications per capita (WIPO)
- Unicorn company count
- Nobel Prize output (trailing 20yr)
- AI/deep tech index (Stanford HAI)

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 9/10 | ↓ slightly | Still #1 in frontier AI, biotech, software; but China closing gap rapidly |
| China | 7/10 | ↑ | Patent volume now > US; AI investment; quantum; but quality gap remains at frontier |
| Korea | 6/10 | → | Samsung/TSMC adjacency; semiconductors; but lacks software/AI frontier |

---

## Measure 3 — Competitiveness

### Definition
Ability of businesses and industries to compete globally on cost, quality, and scale. Encompasses labor productivity, regulatory efficiency, and business environment.

### How to Quantify
- World Economic Forum Global Competitiveness Index
- World Bank Ease of Doing Business
- Labor productivity per hour (OECD)
- Export complexity (Harvard Growth Lab ECI)

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 7/10 | ↓ | Still competitive in tech/finance; manufacturing hollowed; regulatory burden rising |
| China | 7/10 | ↑ | Manufacturing dominance; scale; but innovation-competitiveness transition incomplete |
| Korea | 7/10 | → | Semiconductors, shipbuilding, EVs competitive; chaebol rigidity limits agility |

---

## Measure 4 — Economic Output

### Definition
Absolute and relative size of the economy. Dalio uses both nominal GDP and purchasing power parity (PPP) GDP.

### How to Quantify
- Nominal GDP (IMF WEO)
- GDP PPP (IMF)
- GDP per capita (nominal and PPP)
- Share of world GDP

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 8/10 | ↓ | ~25% of world GDP nominal; still #1 nominal but China PPP now larger |
| China | 9/10 | ↑ | #1 in PPP terms (~18% nominal, ~19% PPP); still #2 nominal |
| Korea | 5/10 | → | ~1.6% world GDP; #12-13 globally; punches above weight per capita |

---

## Measure 5 — World Trade Share

### Definition
Share of global imports and exports. A dominant trading nation can project influence via trade relationships and set standards.

### How to Quantify
- Exports % of world total (WTO)
- Imports % of world total
- Trade network centrality (World Bank)
- Number of active free trade agreements

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 7/10 | ↓ | ~10-11% world trade; still large but declining relative share; nearshoring trend |
| China | 9/10 | ↑ | ~15% world trade; #1 trading partner for 120+ countries |
| Korea | 5/10 | → | ~3% world trade; highly export-dependent (trade/GDP ~80%) |

---

## Measure 6 — Military Strength

### Definition
Capacity to project force globally, defend territory, and deter rivals. Dalio includes nuclear deterrence, conventional force, and power projection capability.

### How to Quantify
- Defense spending (SIPRI)
- Defense spending % GDP
- Nuclear warhead count
- Naval tonnage and carrier groups
- Global military base count

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 9/10 | ↓ | Still #1; 800+ overseas bases; 11 carrier groups; nuclear arsenal; but budget stress |
| China | 7/10 | ↑ | Rapid modernization; largest navy by ship count; regional power projection strong |
| Korea | 5/10 | → | Capable regional force; US alliance; North Korea threat; limited global projection |

---

## Measure 7 — Financial Center Status

### Definition
Being home to dominant global financial markets, institutions, and capital allocation mechanisms. Enables seigniorage and influence over global capital flows.

### How to Quantify
- Global Financial Centres Index (GFCI)
- Stock market capitalization % of world total
- Banking system size (BIS)
- Foreign investment in domestic markets

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 9/10 | ↓ slightly | NYSE + Nasdaq = ~44% world market cap; Wall Street dominant; but rivals growing |
| China | 6/10 | ↑ | Shanghai/Shenzhen now #3-4 globally; capital controls limit internationalization |
| Korea | 4/10 | → | KOSPI significant regionally; not a global financial center |

---

## Measure 8 — Reserve Currency Status

### Definition
The degree to which a nation's currency is held by central banks worldwide as a store of value and used in international trade invoicing. The "exorbitant privilege."

### How to Quantify
- IMF COFER: USD share of global reserves
- SWIFT payment share
- Oil and commodity invoicing currency
- IMF SDR basket weight

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 9/10 | ↓ | USD ~58% of global reserves (down from 71% in 2001); still dominant but declining |
| China | 4/10 | ↑ | RMB ~2-3% of reserves; internationalizing slowly; capital controls are ceiling |
| Korea | 2/10 | → | KRW not a reserve currency; USD-linked in practice |

---

## Measure 9 — Debt Level

### Definition
The burden of accumulated debt relative to income (GDP). High debt constrains future spending, forces monetization, and creates fragility.

### How to Quantify
- General government debt % GDP (IMF)
- Total economy debt (public + private + corporate) % GDP (IIF)
- Debt service / government revenue ratio

### Scores (Higher debt = LOWER score in Dalio's framework)

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 3/10 | ↓ | Federal debt ~120% GDP; total economy debt ~360% GDP; unsustainable trajectory |
| China | 4/10 | ↓ | Official government debt ~55% GDP but local government + SOE debt ~300% total |
| Korea | 6/10 | ↓ | Government debt ~50% GDP; household debt very high (~100% GDP — risk) |

---

## Measure 10 — Money Creation

### Definition
The pace and scale of central bank money printing. Excessive money creation erodes currency value and signals inability to fund obligations through taxes/borrowing.

### How to Quantify
- Central bank balance sheet size / GDP
- M2 growth rate YoY
- Real interest rates (negative = financial repression)
- Inflation rate (CPI)

### Scores (Excessive creation = LOWER score)

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 4/10 | ↓ | Fed balance sheet ~$7T after peak $9T; QT underway but next crisis = more QE |
| China | 5/10 | ↓ | PBOC expanding balance sheet; property crisis response; credit growth elevated |
| Korea | 6/10 | → | BOK moderate; inflation under control; but exposed to global financial conditions |

---

## Measure 11 — Wealth/Income Gap

### Definition
The degree of inequality within the society. Wide gaps create populist backlash, reduce social trust, and eventually generate internal conflict.

### How to Quantify
- Gini coefficient (World Inequality Database)
- Top 1% / bottom 50% wealth ratio
- Social mobility index (World Economic Forum)
- Wage growth vs. capital returns comparison

### Scores (High inequality = LOWER score)

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 3/10 | ↓ | Gini ~0.49 (one of highest in developed world); top 1% owns ~38% of wealth |
| China | 4/10 | ↓ | Gini ~0.47; rapid rise of ultra-wealthy alongside rural poverty; Xi's "common prosperity" response |
| Korea | 5/10 | ↓ | Gini ~0.35; better than US but chaebol concentration; youth precariat rising |

---

## Measure 12 — Internal Conflict

### Definition
The degree to which citizens are fighting each other — class conflict, political polarization, civil unrest. Dalio views this as a leading indicator of empire decline.

### How to Quantify
- Political polarization index (Pew Research)
- Social unrest/protest events (ACLED)
- Election integrity perception (Varieties of Democracy)
- Media trust index

### Scores (High conflict = LOWER score)

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 3/10 | ↓ | Political polarization at modern highs; January 6 analog; trust in institutions collapsed |
| China | 5/10 | → | Suppressed externally; internal discontent (youth unemployment, property crisis) |
| Korea | 6/10 | → | 2024-2025 political crisis (impeachment); polarized but institutions functioned |

---

## Measure 13 — Internal Order

### Definition
The effectiveness of government in maintaining social order, rule of law, and institutional function. Different from internal conflict — this is about state capacity.

### How to Quantify
- Rule of Law Index (World Justice Project)
- Government effectiveness (World Bank Governance Indicators)
- Corruption Perception Index (Transparency International)
- State capacity measures

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 6/10 | ↓ | Rule of law still strong at federal level; but norms erosion; executive overreach risk |
| China | 6/10 | → | High state capacity; low corruption at federal level; low civil liberty offset |
| Korea | 7/10 | → | Strong institutions; democratic backsliding risk flagged but recovered 2025 |

---

## Measure 14 — External Conflict

### Definition
The degree to which the nation is engaged in or at risk of external military conflict. Wars drain resources, disrupt trade, and accelerate debt cycles.

### How to Quantify
- Active military engagements (SIPRI)
- Alliance strength (treaty network)
- Proxy war involvement
- Border dispute intensity

### Scores (High external conflict = LOWER score)

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 5/10 | ↓ | NATO commitments; Ukraine/Taiwan flashpoints; Middle East entanglement; overstretch |
| China | 5/10 | ↓ | Taiwan strait risk; South China Sea disputes; India border; increasing proxy involvement |
| Korea | 5/10 | ↓ | North Korea threat acute; US alliance provides deterrence; but proximity to China-Taiwan risk |

---

## Measure 15 — Education Quality

### Definition
Dalio distinguishes this from Measure 1 (breadth). Measure 15 focuses specifically on quality of output — critical thinking, creativity, and applied problem-solving vs. rote memorization.

### How to Quantify
- PISA subscores (specifically reasoning/problem-solving)
- Global Innovation Index (WIPO)
- Academic output quality (citation-weighted papers, Nature Index)

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 7/10 | ↓ | Elite universities still world-class; but K-12 critical thinking weak; student debt crisis |
| China | 6/10 | ↑ | Top in STEM output; improving research quality; but creativity/critical thinking constrained by system |
| Korea | 7/10 | ↑ | Strong STEM quality; KAIST/POSTECH competitive globally; rote learning reform underway |

---

## Measure 16 — Productivity

### Definition
Output per unit of input — labor productivity growth, total factor productivity. The engine of real wealth creation. Dalio views slowing productivity as a structural warning sign.

### How to Quantify
- Labor productivity per hour (OECD/BLS)
- Total factor productivity growth (Penn World Tables)
- GDP per worker (World Bank)

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 7/10 | → | AI-driven productivity revival possible; services-heavy economy; manufacturing productivity high |
| China | 6/10 | ↑ | Moving up value chain; manufacturing productivity growing; services still developing |
| Korea | 7/10 | → | High manufacturing productivity; services sector lagging; AI adoption accelerating |

---

## Measure 17 — Geological / Natural Advantages

### Definition
Natural resource endowment, geographic security, agricultural potential, and climate advantages. Nations with natural resource wealth and geographic protection have structural advantages in decline periods.

### How to Quantify
- Natural resource rents % GDP (World Bank)
- Arable land per capita
- Energy self-sufficiency ratio
- Geographic isolation / defense ease

### Scores

| Nation | Score | Trend | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| US | 8/10 | → | Massive oil/gas reserves; agricultural heartland; ocean moats; energy independent |
| China | 5/10 | ↓ | Rare earths dominant; but limited oil/gas; water scarcity; agricultural land pressure |
| Korea | 3/10 | ↓ | Resource-poor; energy fully import-dependent; small territory; geographically exposed |
